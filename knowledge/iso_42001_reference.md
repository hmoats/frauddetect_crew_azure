# ISO 42001 AI Management System Reference

## Overview
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and
continually improving an AI management system (AIMS) within organizations. It applies to
any organization providing or using AI-based products or services.

## Key Clauses Relevant to Fraud Detection Systems

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization
- Identify internal/external issues relevant to AI systems
- Understand needs and expectations of interested parties (regulators, customers, auditors)
- Define the scope of the AIMS

### Clause 5: Leadership
- Top management commitment to AI governance
- Establish AI policy aligned with organizational strategy
- Define roles, responsibilities, and authorities for AI oversight

### Clause 6: Planning
- AI risk assessment: identify risks from AI system decisions (false positives/negatives)
- AI impact assessment: evaluate societal, ethical, and legal impacts
- Define objectives for AI system performance, fairness, and transparency

### Clause 7: Support
- Provide resources for AI system development, monitoring, and maintenance
- Ensure competence of personnel operating AI systems
- Maintain awareness of AI governance policies
- Document information about AI system design, training data, and decision processes

### Clause 8: Operation
- Operational planning and control for AI systems
- AI system lifecycle management: design, development, deployment, monitoring, retirement
- Data management: quality, provenance, bias assessment, privacy
- Third-party AI component management

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
- Monitor, measure, analyze, and evaluate AI system performance
- Conduct internal audits of the AIMS
- Management review of AI governance effectiveness
- Track metrics: accuracy, fairness, drift, explainability

### Clause 10: Improvement
- Address nonconformities and take corrective action
- Continual improvement of AI management system
- Incident management for AI system failures or unintended outcomes

## Transparency and Explainability Requirements
- Document the purpose and intended use of AI systems
- Provide explanations of AI decisions to affected parties
- Maintain records of model training data, algorithms, and performance metrics
- Enable human review and override of AI decisions

## Human Oversight Requirements
- Define escalation paths for AI decisions requiring human judgment
- Implement human-in-the-loop for high-risk decisions
- Provide training for human reviewers on AI system capabilities and limitations
- Maintain audit trails of human override decisions

## Data Governance Requirements
- Document data sources, quality standards, and preprocessing steps
- Assess and mitigate data bias
- Implement data protection and privacy controls
- Maintain data lineage and provenance records

## Risk Management Framework
- Classify AI system risk levels (low, medium, high, critical)
- Implement controls proportional to risk level
- Regular risk reassessment as systems and environments change
- Document risk treatment decisions and residual risks
